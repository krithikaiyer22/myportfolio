CAPSTONE PROJECT · AUG 2025 – DEC 2025

Pension Fund Investment Strategy

The Problem

The plan started in January 2010 with $5.0B in assets against $5.9B in liabilities — an initial funded ratio of 84.7% and a funding deficit of $0.9B. The sponsor needed to close this gap using investment returns in a controlled risk framework, rather than contributions alone. The challenge: how to transform an underfunded plan into a surplus position without taking on risks that could produce large drawdowns in funded status.

Why This Approach

Rather than optimizing assets in isolation, we framed the entire problem as surplus optimization — assets minus liabilities. This changed every decision. A long-duration fixed income allocation of 35% wasn't about earning yield; it was about hedging the plan's 13-year liability duration. TIPS (10% of portfolio) were included not for return, but to protect the real value of promised benefits against inflation. The quality-biased equity sleeve (SPY, EFA, EEM, QUAL — totaling 35%) was sized to generate the growth needed to close the funding gap, while its downside was capped by SPY collar overlays.

The FX hedge policy — 50% EUR hedge and 40% JPY hedge via rolling forwards — was chosen because backtesting from 2015–2025 showed FX exposure mainly contributed volatility without improving long-run returns. Partial hedging reduced surplus noise at low cost. JPY tail-risk calls were layered in because the yen historically appreciates in risk-off episodes — exactly when the pension sponsor most needs protection.

Empirical Results (2010–2025)

The portfolio delivered 8.7% annualized returns with 7.4% volatility and a Sharpe ratio of 1.18. Assets grew to $18.28B; liabilities stayed near $5.84B; the funded ratio rose from 84.7% to 313%, with a surplus of $12.44B. The worst drawdown of –19.8% (2022) never pushed the funded ratio below its starting level, because rising discount rates simultaneously reduced the present value of liabilities — confirming that surplus-space analysis gives a more accurate picture of economic risk than asset-only metrics.

Stress Tests

Under stagflation, assets fell but liabilities fell further (discount rates rose sharply), improving the funded ratio to 96.7%. A global recession with falling rates was the worst outcome — funded ratio dropped to 69.3% — because declining discount rates simultaneously inflated liabilities. A soft landing brought the plan to near full funding at 99.9%. These scenarios confirmed that the liability discount rate, not just equity performance, drives funded-status outcomes.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel (Advanced Financial Modeling), Yahoo Finance data pipeline (monthly adjusted close prices, 2010–2025), Bloomberg Terminal, Python for data validation and returns engineering, Chart.js for interactive visualization.

CAPSTONE PROJECT · OCT 2023 – APR 2024

MSME Financial Research – India

The Problem

MSMEs in India represent over 30% of GDP but remain chronically underserved by formal credit markets — largely because lenders lack reliable financial data to assess their risk. The core issue isn't that these businesses are necessarily high-risk; it's that the data used to evaluate them is inconsistent, unreconciled, and often misleading. Poor data quality leads to either over-denial of credit (leaving viable businesses unfunded) or mispriced risk (leading to unexpected defaults).

Why This Approach

The research prioritized data validation and reconciliation before any risk analysis — because building a risk model on dirty data produces confident but wrong conclusions. Most financial analyses skip this step or treat it as administrative. Here, it was treated as the analytical foundation: until we knew what the numbers actually meant, no risk scoring was meaningful.

A combined quantitative and qualitative framework was chosen because MSME financials don't tell the full story alone. A manufacturing firm with volatile revenue might be perfectly healthy if its cash conversion cycle is tight and its customers are stable — context that pure ratio analysis misses. Layering qualitative insights onto quantitative signals allowed for more accurate risk classification than either method could achieve alone.

Risk profiling was structured across four dimensions — credit, liquidity, operational, and market risk — because each represents a distinct type of failure mode. Treating them separately made it possible to identify which businesses were exposed to which specific risks, rather than collapsing everything into a single score that obscures the nature of the problem.

Outcome & Impact

The reconciliation process surfaced meaningful discrepancies across the dataset, ultimately improving usable data accuracy to 73%. Credit risk was identified as the dominant concern (72% of entities), but the disaggregated analysis revealed that many of those flagged for credit risk were actually solvent — their issue was liquidity timing, not fundamental insolvency. That distinction matters enormously for how a lender or investor should respond: a liquidity problem is solvable with structured credit; an insolvency problem is not.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel (Data Validation, Reconciliation, Pivot Tables, VLOOKUP, Financial Modeling), Qualitative Analysis frameworks, structured Risk Assessment matrices.

RESEARCH PROJECT · FIN 494/591 FINTECH/BLOCKCHAIN · ASU W.P. CAREY

Reddit Sentiment Cascades & Meme-Stock Volatility

The Problem

The 2021 meme-stock phenomenon — GME, AMC, BB, KOSS, NOK — raised a real empirical question: does Reddit's r/WallStreetBets actually contain predictive information about near-term volatility, or is it noise? Most prior research collapsed sentiment into daily averages and used opaque data pipelines, making results hard to reproduce or act on.

Approach & Methodology

We built a fully reproducible Python pipeline processing ~4,890 thirty-minute windows across 6 tickers (Jan–Aug 2021). For each window we computed VADER sentiment scores weighted by engagement (upvotes + comments), posting volume, sentiment z-scores, rolling tail-share (share of extreme windows over last 20), and session/macro context labels. Forward 5-day realized volatility was the outcome variable, linked via Yahoo Finance price data.

Key Findings

Posting volume beats raw sentiment — the posts-per-minute coefficient was 4.62 (p≈4.7e-40), the strongest predictor in the logistic model. Persistent extremes matter more than one-off spikes — rising tail-share from 0.10 to 0.30 nearly doubles high-volatility odds (coef: 2.12, p=3.6e-4). Macro events amplify everything — macro-tagged windows had a 42% high-vol hit rate vs. 24.9% for normal windows. Most strikingly, simultaneous sentiment + volume spikes signal exhaustion, not takeoff — the interaction term was −2.86 (p≈3e-12), with high-vol rate collapsing to just 4.9% when both spike together. Volatility also cooled ~12% within two days after a sentiment spike.

Tools Used

Python (pandas, VADER v3.3.2, scikit-learn, matplotlib), Yahoo Finance, Kaggle WSB dataset, logistic regression and event-study analysis.

FAC336 WORKING CAPITAL MANAGEMENT · AHMEDABAD UNIVERSITY

Working Capital Analysis — Ponni Sugars (Erode) Ltd.

The Problem

Ponni Sugars (Erode) Limited, an Indian sugar manufacturer, showed a mixed financial trajectory over 2021–2023. The central question was whether its working capital management was keeping pace with its rapid revenue growth — and what the risk signals were for short-term liquidity and operational sustainability.

Analysis

We analyzed five years of P&L and balance sheet data (Mar '19–Mar '23) across all core working capital components: inventories, trade receivables, cash, payables, and short-term provisions. Key ratios computed included Current Ratio (3.0x in 2023), Quick Ratio (1.28x), Inventory Turnover (4.62x), Net Working Capital Ratio (0.19), and DSO/DPO cycles. The working capital cycle and positive vs. negative capital dynamics were evaluated against industry context and the company's seasonal sugar-crushing business model.

Key Findings

Working capital grew from ₹76.3Cr to ₹96.4Cr (2021–2023), but the 4.67% increase in 2022–23 lagged the 48.9% revenue surge, suggesting increasing pressure on cash conversion. DSO fell sharply from 57.5 to 18.9 days — a positive efficiency signal — while DPO also declined from 70.1 to 45.5 days, meaning the company was paying suppliers faster than before. The Quick Ratio declining from 1.57 to 1.28 flagged growing inventory concentration risk. The company became debt-free by 2022 (total debt = ₹0), strengthening its credit profile significantly.

Recommendations

Optimise inventory levels to improve quick ratio liquidity; negotiate longer supplier payment terms to widen the cash conversion gap; consider revolving credit facilities to buffer seasonal production peaks without eroding working capital efficiency.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel (ratio analysis, trend modeling), BSE India financial disclosures, Dion Global Solutions financial data.

CIS 505 TEAM PROJECT · ASU W.P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

Nike Promotional Analytics & Database Design

The Problem

Nike reported a 9% global sales drop in Q3 FY2025, its steepest decline in nearly five years, despite aggressive discounting. App downloads fell 35%, in-store foot traffic dropped 11%, and digital sales declined 15%. The core issue: Nike lacked clear visibility into which customer segments, product categories, and channels were delivering the strongest return on promotional investment — and which discounts were driving volume versus margin erosion.

Database Design

Our team designed a 7-table relational database schema (Sales, Customer, Product, DSG_Data, Retailer, Marketing, Review) with an EER diagram mapping relationships across Nike's direct-to-consumer and partner retail channels. The schema supported analysis across price elasticity, demographic segmentation, sentiment, and limited-edition purchasing behavior.

Key Analytical Findings

Price elasticity: The 10–20% discount range generated the highest revenue ($2.60M), outperforming both no-discount ($1.76M) and deep 40–50% discounts ($1.62M), indicating that moderate promotions are most efficient. Segment targeting: Younger, non-loyalty customers in urban markets (Dallas, Phoenix) showed the highest promo response rates. Limited-edition buyers: Middle-aged customers (45–64) spending an average of $687 with 2.3 orders per customer represent Nike's most valuable repeat segment. Sentiment: Running shoes and lifestyle sneakers led in both rating and review volume, while cleats underperformed — pointing to design or expectation gaps.

Recommendations

Apply deep discounts selectively during clearance periods; preserve full-price positioning for loyal segments through early access programs; prioritize limited-edition launches in specialty retail with targeted online expansion; align marketing spend with high-rated product lines to amplify brand trust.

Tools Used

MySQL (schema design, sample queries), AI-generated simulated Nike sales dataset, EER diagramming, SQL analytics (SELECT, JOIN, CASE, GROUP BY, CTEs).
